1. Introduction

ReagenTree is a web application designed to manage laboratory reagents and stocks and keeps track of the reagent inventory, alerting the users when stocks are running low. ReagenTree is webbrowser and operation system angnostic and mobile-friendly, meaning it can be accessed from any device with a web browser including mobile devices. ReagenTree is powered by Django, JavaScript, and Bootstrap, the entire code base is open source and can be found on GitHub.

2. User Account

2.1. Registration

An account is required to use ReagenTree. To register an account, click on the register link on the login page and fill in the registration form. The registration form requires you to provide your email address, first name, last name, and password. Follow the instructions on the registration form to complete the registration process. Please note, only an OUH email address is accepted. A new user account requires approval from the administrator users.

2.2. Login

To login to ReagenTree, click on the login link on the home page and enter your email address and password. If you have forgotten your password, contact the administrator users to reset your password.

2.3. Logout

User sessions automatically after 15 minutes of inactivity. Any further actions after your session expired will redirect your to the Login page for re-authentication. To logout manually, click on the Logout link under the user menu.

2.4. Account previleges

There are two types of users in ReagenTree: regular users and administrators. If you find that you cannot perform certain actions, contact the administrator users to grant you the necessary permissions.

3. Model Concepts

3.1. Item

An item is a generic term used to describe a reagent, a stock, or any other item that can be stored in a laboratory. An item has a name, a category, and a location.

3.2. Reagent (in-house reagent)

A reagent is a chemical compound used in a laboratory to detect, measure, or produce other substances. A reagent has a name, a category, a location, a company, and a CAS number.

3.3. Stock

A stock is a container that holds a reagent. A stock has a name, a category, a location, a company, a CAS number, and a quantity. A stock can be linked to a reagent.

4. Settings

4.1. Companies

Companies are entities that produce reagents. Companies have a name and a website. Companies can be linked to reagents and stocks.

4.2. Categories

Categories are used to classify items. Categories have a name and a description. Categories can be linked to reagents and stocks.

4.3. Locations

Locations are places where items are stored. Locations have a name and a description. Locations can be linked to reagents and stocks.

4.4. Other settings

Other settings include the ability to change your password, view your profile, and view the audit log.

5. How to

5.1. Add a new supplier or manufacturer company

To add a new company, click on the New Company button on the company page and fill in the company form. The company form requires you to provide the company name and website. Follow the instructions on the company form to complete the company creation process.
