User Guide

1. Introduction

ReagenTree is a web application designed to manage laboratory reagents and stocks and keeps track of the reagent inventory, alerting the users when stocks are running low. ReagenTree is webbrowser and operation system angnostic and mobile-friendly, meaning it can be accessed from any device with a web browser including mobile devices. ReagenTree is powered by Django, JavaScript, and Bootstrap, the entire code base is open source and can be found on GitHub.

2. User Account

2.1. Registration

An account is required to use ReagenTree. To register an account, click on the register link on the login page and fill in the registration form. The registration form requires you to provide your email address, first name, last name, and password. Follow the instructions on the registration form to complete the registration process. Please note, only an OUH email address is accepted. A new user account requires approval from an administrator user.

2.2. Login

To login to ReagenTree, click on the login link on the home page and enter your email address and password. If you have forgotten your password, contact the administrator users to reset your password.

2.3. Logout

User sessions automatically after 15 minutes of inactivity. Any further actions after your session expired will redirect your to the Login page for re-authentication. To logout manually, click on the Logout link under the user menu.

2.4. Account previleges

There are two types of users in ReagenTree: regular users and administrators. If you find that you cannot perform certain actions, contact the administrator users to grant you the necessary permissions.

3. Data Models

3.1. Item

In Reagentree, an item is a generic term used to refer to a reagent which can be procured from a supplier. Think of an item as a dish listed on a restaurant menu, not as the actual dish served to you, which would be considered as a stock.

3.2. Reagent (in-house)

For lack of a better term, Reagentree uses reagent to refer to a reagent that is produced in-house. In terms of the data model, a reagent behaves the same as an item - it is also like a dish on a menu, not the dish served, which would be considered stock. Therefore, a new reagent cannot be created directly from items or other reagents. A reagent must be created from one or more stock items.

3.3. Stock

In Reagentree, stock refers to the actual physical inventory of a reagent. While an item or reagent is like a dish listed on a restaurant menu, stock is the actual dish served to you. Stock represents the tangible, on-hand quantity of reagents, whether procured from a supplier or produced in-house. It tracks the availability and usage of reagents, ensuring that the necessary quantities are maintained for operational needs.

4. Settings

4.1. Companies

A company refers to an entity involved in the manufacturing or supply of reagents. A company can function as either a manufacturer, a supplier, or both.

4.2. Categories

In Reagentree, categories are used to classify items and reagents. Each category has a searchable name and a description, helping in the efficient sorting and retrieval of information.

4.3. Locations

Locations are places where stocks are stored. Each location has a searchable name and a description. Locations help in tracking and managing the physical storage of stock items,

4.4. User profile settings

User profile information such as first name, last name, and email address can be updated by clicking on the Profile option under the user menu.

5. How to

5.1. Add a new supplier or manufacturer company

To add a new company, click on the New Company button on the company page and fill in the company form. The company form requires you to provide the company name and website. Follow the instructions on the company form to complete the company creation process.
